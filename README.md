# Telecom Domain Insights — AtliQo 5G Launch Analysis

> **A data-driven investigation into what happened to AtliQo's business after launching 5G — and what they should do about it.**

---

## Project Overview

AtliQo, a major telecom provider, launched 5G services in **May 2022**. What followed was unexpected: a noticeable **drop in active users** and **slower revenue growth**. This project delivers a full pre vs. post 5G launch analysis across KPIs — uncovering what went wrong, which cities were hit hardest, which plans survived, and what strategic actions can reverse the decline.

## Key Findings

### What Went Wrong?

**1. Pricing Was Too High**
5G plan prices were not competitive, causing a significant portion of customers to reconsider their subscriptions.

**2. Mass User Exodus in Metro Cities**
Big cities like **Delhi** and **Mumbai** — AtliQo's highest-value markets — lost the most active users post-launch.

**3. Unsubscriptions Spiked by 13 Lakh**
> Unsubscribed users increased by **13 Lakhs** after 5G launch — a clear signal of customer dissatisfaction with the new service or its cost.

**4. Revenue Dropped in Key Cities**
Revenue loss was most concentrated in **Delhi**, **Chennai**, and **Ahmedabad**.

**5. Market Share Eroded**
| Period | Market Share |
|--------|-------------|
| March (Pre-5G Peak) | ~21% |
| September (Post-5G) | 17.84% |

> **Net drop: ~3% in market share within months of 5G launch.**

## Plan Performance Insights

### Before 5G Launch
- **Smart Recharge Pack (P1)** was the top earner at ₹181.27
- Plans with **1GB–2GB/day** data were most popular
- Small top-up packs (P8, P9, P10) contributed the least revenue

### After 5G Launch
- **P1** remained #1 with revenue jumping to ₹238.66
- **Ultra Fast Mega Pack (P11)** emerged as a strong new entry — immediately becoming the 2nd highest earner
- High-data 5G plans (P11, P12) began replacing older, lower-value options
- Revenue from basic plans like the **₹99 pack (P5)** fell sharply as users shifted to 5G tiers

---

## Recommendations

### 1. Introduce Mid-Tier Plans
Bridge the gap between P1 and P11 to win back millions of price-sensitive users who switched to cheaper competitors.

### 2. Replicate the Pune Success Model
> Pune was the **only city to gain massive users (+12 Lakh)** post-5G.

Investigate whether Pune benefited from special promotions or better network stability — then apply that model in Delhi and Mumbai.

### 3. Win Back Unsubscribed Users
- Target the **13 lakh users who left**
- Offer re-engagement discounts or special loyalty deals
- Provide cheaper 5G plans or device bundling offers
- Make re-subscription simple and attractive

### 4. Retain High-Value Metro Customers
Delhi and Mumbai drive the most revenue. Offer **loyalty rewards or bundled 5G services** (e.g., free streaming) to prevent existing high-value users from switching to competitors.

### 5. Strengthen the 4G/5G Bridge
> Not all users have 5G-capable devices yet. Avoid forcing them onto expensive plans.

Keep strong 4G options alive to maintain total active user count while 5G adoption matures.
